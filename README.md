# Superstore Sales Performance Dashboard | Excel

> **Business Question:** Which product categories and regions are dragging down profit — and what should the business stop discounting?



##  The Problem

The Superstore dataset contains 4 years of sales data across thousands of transactions, regions, and product lines. On the surface, $2.3M in sales looks healthy. But buried in the data is a profitability crisis: certain product lines are actively losing money, and aggressive discounting is the primary cause.

This dashboard surfaces those issues clearly so a sales or ops team can act on them.

##  Key Business Findings

**1. Furniture is the silent profit killer.**
Technology generates the highest profit margin. Furniture generates significant sales volume but near-zero — and sometimes negative — profit. Tables specifically run at a loss. The business is investing fulfilment and warehouse resources into a category that costs more than it earns.

**2. Discounts above 20% destroy profitability across all categories.**
The discount vs. profit analysis shows a clear negative correlation. There is no threshold at which high discounts become profitable. The current discounting strategy is reducing margin faster than it is growing volume.

**3. The Consumer segment drives revenue but Corporate has better margins.**
Consumer segment leads in both sales and orders. However, Corporate and Home Office segments, while smaller, show stronger profit-per-order. The business may be under-serving its most efficient customer type.

**4. 5 sub-categories generate the majority of profit.**
Copiers, Phones, Accessories, Paper, and Binders account for a disproportionate share of profit. The bottom 5 sub-categories (Tables, Bookcases, Supplies) consistently underperform or lose money.

---

##  Business Recommendations

1. **Stop discounting Tables.** This sub-category generates losses even at full price. Discounting it further accelerates losses. Either reprice, reduce SKU count, or exit the category.

2. **Cap discounts at 15% company-wide.** The data shows profitability breaks down above this threshold. A discount policy with a hard ceiling would immediately improve margin without reducing order volume.

3. **Invest more in the Technology category.** It has the strongest margin, consistent demand, and room to scale. Marketing and stock investment should shift toward this category.

4. **Explore Corporate segment growth.** Corporate buyers have smaller volume but better unit economics. A dedicated B2B sales effort could improve overall margin without increasing order complexity.
